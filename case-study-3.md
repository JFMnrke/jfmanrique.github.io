# Case Study 3

## Building High-Trust Teams & Driving Organizational Change

### Overview

While working across large-scale advertising operations, I identified an opportunity to improve not only operational efficiency but also collaboration, onboarding, and knowledge sharing across teams.

Rather than approaching the challenge as a documentation project alone, I assembled a small cross-functional team focused on identifying operational pain points, improving internal processes, and creating resources that would help both new and existing team members perform more effectively.

The initiative evolved into a broader effort that combined documentation, onboarding support, employee wellbeing initiatives, and organizational change management.

### The Challenge

Teams across multiple regions operated with different processes, documentation standards, and workflows.

New hires often faced a steep learning curve, relying heavily on verbal knowledge transfer and fragmented documentation to understand campaign activation processes.

At the same time, operational challenges created unnecessary complexity for activation teams, increasing onboarding time and reducing efficiency.

The challenge was not simply to create documentation, but to create alignment across people, processes, and regions.

---

## My Approach

I personally recruited and led a small team of collaborators from different operational areas, bringing together individuals with strong curiosity, collaboration skills, and a genuine interest in improving the way we worked.

Together we:

* Audited existing workflows and onboarding processes
* Documented complex campaign activation procedures
* Created training resources for new team members
* Established recurring planning and review sessions to track initiatives and progress
* Identified operational bottlenecks affecting multiple regions
* Collected feedback directly from team members and stakeholders
* Collaborated with an employee wellbeing initiative to align operational improvements with employee experience goals
* Facilitated conversations between teams with different priorities and working styles to build consensus around proposed changes

Throughout the process, my role focused on research, coordination, stakeholder management, change advocacy, and project leadership.

---

## Mentorship & Onboarding

In addition to leading improvement initiatives, I personally onboarded and trained multiple team members across different accounts and business units.

My mentoring approach focused on more than task execution.

I aimed to help new team members understand:

* Why processes existed
* How different systems connected together
* How to evaluate their own work independently
* How to build confidence when navigating uncertainty

Several team members progressed from requiring close support during onboarding to becoming fully independent contributors and advancing professionally within the industry.

---

## Results

The initiative produced both operational and people-focused outcomes:

* Improved onboarding experience for new team members
* Greater consistency in process adoption across regions
* Better knowledge sharing through structured documentation
* Stronger collaboration between teams that previously worked in silos
* Increased stakeholder alignment during process improvement projects
* Successful adoption of new operational standards across multiple regions
* Development and mentoring of team members who later advanced into new opportunities and responsibilities

---

## Key Takeaway

### Meaningful organizational change begins with listening.

One of the most important lessons from this experience was that lasting improvements rarely come from imposing solutions.

The most effective changes emerged from understanding how people worked, where they struggled, and what they needed in order to succeed.

By combining operational thinking with a people-first approach, I was able to help drive improvements that benefited both business outcomes and team effectiveness.
